Welcome to watching the numbers.

Introduction

Who?

All of these numbers and statistics represent the many fallen of World War Two. They include military and civilian casualties.

Why?

With history seemingly repeating itself lately, we may need to be reminded of the horrific consequences of war.

When?

All of the information provied will begin in the year 1937, to the war's end in 1945.

European Theatre

The European theatre of World War Two was one of the two main theatres of combat during the war. It saw heavy fighting across Europe for almost six years, starting with Germany's invasion of Poland on 1 September 1939 and ending with the Western Allies conquering most of Western Europe. It is difficult to represent such vast numbers on such a small space. The Soviet people suffered a catastrophic amount of losses.

Asian Theatre

The Pacific War, sometimes called the Asia–Pacific War, was the theater of World War Two that was fought in eastern Asia, the Pacific Ocean, the Indian Ocean, and Oceania. It was geographically the largest theatre of the war, including the vast Pacific Ocean theatre, the South West Pacific theatre, the Second Sino-Japanese War, and the Soviet–Japanese War.
